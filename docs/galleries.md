# Photo & Video Galleries

Galleries let you show a collection of photos and videos on a page. Visitors
see them as a tidy grid of thumbnails. When they click one, it opens
full-screen and they can click (or use the arrow keys) to page through the
rest.

There are two steps:

1. **Build a gallery** — collect the photos and videos in one place.
2. **Add it to a page** — drop a **Media Gallery** block onto any page and
   pick the gallery you built.

You only build a gallery once. You can then show the same gallery on as many
pages as you like.

---

## Step 1: Build a gallery

1. In the admin sidebar, under **Content**, click **Galleries**.
2. Click **Create New**.
3. Give it a **Title**. This is just a label so you can find the gallery
   later — visitors never see it. Something like "Season Highlights 2026"
   works well.
4. Add your photos and videos under **Items** (see below).
5. Click **Save**.

### Adding items

Each photo or video is one **Item**. Click **Add Item** for each one. For
every item, choose what kind it is:

- **Image or video upload** — upload a photo or video file from your
  computer. This is the option for most photos.
- **Video link (YouTube, Vimeo)** — paste a link to a video that already
  lives on YouTube or Vimeo, instead of uploading a file.

**If you chose "Image or video upload":**

- Pick the file under **Media** (upload a new one or choose an existing one).

**If you chose "Video link":**

- **Video URL** — paste the link to the video, for example
  `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=…` or `https://vimeo.com/…`.
  Only YouTube and Vimeo links work.
- **Video title** — a short description of the video (e.g. "Avalanche control
  at Snoqualmie Pass"). This is read aloud by screen readers, so please fill
  it in — it helps people who can't see the video.

**For any item:**

- **Caption** — optional. If you add one, it shows underneath the item when a
  visitor opens it full-screen. It does not show on the thumbnail grid.

You can drag items up and down to reorder them. The order here is the order
visitors see.

> **Tip:** A gallery needs at least one item before you can save it.

---

## Step 2: Add the gallery to a page

1. Open the page where you want the gallery to appear.
2. Add a new block and choose **Media Gallery**.
3. Set the options:
   - **Gallery** — choose the gallery you built in Step 1. (You'll only see
     galleries from your own center.)
   - **Layout** — how the thumbnails are arranged:
     - **Grid** — even rows and columns, all the same size.
     - **Masonry** — a Pinterest-style layout where tiles keep their natural
       shape and fit together.
   - **Columns** — how many thumbnails sit across: **2**, **3**, or **4**
     (4 is the default). Fewer columns means bigger thumbnails.
   - **Description** — optional text shown above the gallery. You can include
     links here.
4. Save/publish the page.

That's it. Visitors will see the grid, and clicking any thumbnail opens the
full-screen viewer they can page through.

---

## Good to know

- **Where captions show:** only in the full-screen view, not on the grid.
- **YouTube and Vimeo videos** play right inside the full-screen viewer. When
  a visitor moves to the next item, the video stops.
- **Reusing a gallery:** the same gallery can appear on multiple pages. Edit
  the gallery once and every page that uses it updates automatically.
- **Only YouTube and Vimeo links are supported** for video links. Other
  video sites won't work — upload the file instead, or use YouTube/Vimeo.
